Your Waste Material is Cardboard.

Cardboard waste is one of the most common types of packaging waste and can quickly pile up in landfills if not disposed of properly. When left in damp conditions, it breaks down and can attract pests or grow mold, contributing to environmental pollution. Although cardboard is biodegradable, large amounts of waste still take up space and waste valuable resources.

Recycling cardboard is an easy and effective way to reduce waste. It can be reused to make new packaging, saving trees and energy. Proper disposal, such as flattening boxes and sending them to recycling centers, helps keep the environment clean and reduces the need for cutting down more trees.
