Your Waste Material is Battery.

Batteries are considered hazardous waste due to the toxic chemicals they contain, such as lead, mercury, and lithium. When discarded improperly, they can leak harmful substances into the soil and water, causing pollution and posing risks to human health and wildlife. Additionally, lithium-ion batteries can overheat and catch fire, making them a safety hazard if not disposed of correctly.

To prevent environmental damage, batteries should never be thrown into regular trash. Instead, they should be taken to designated recycling centers or e-waste collection points. Recycling helps recover valuable materials and ensures harmful chemicals are safely handled, reducing pollution and promoting sustainable waste management.
