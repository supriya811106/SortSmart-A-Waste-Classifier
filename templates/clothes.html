Your Waste Material is Clothes.

Clothing waste is a growing problem as old or unwanted clothes often end up in landfills, taking years to decompose. Synthetic fabrics like polyester release microplastics into the environment, while natural fabrics like cotton and wool require large amounts of water and energy to produce. Throwing away clothes instead of reusing or recycling them contributes to pollution and wastes valuable resources.

The best way to manage clothing waste is by reusing, donating, or recycling old clothes. Many fabrics can be repurposed into new garments or used for other materials like insulation. By choosing sustainable options, we can reduce landfill waste, conserve resources, and lessen the impact of fashion on the environment.
