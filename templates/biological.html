Your Waste Material is Biological.

Biological (organic) waste, such as food scraps, leaves, and plant materials, decomposes naturally but can create problems if not managed properly. When dumped in landfills, organic waste breaks down without enough oxygen, producing methane gas, a harmful greenhouse gas that contributes to climate change. Additionally, rotting organic waste can attract pests and cause unpleasant odors.

Composting is the best way to handle organic waste. It turns food and plant scraps into nutrient-rich soil, which can be used for gardening and farming. Proper organic waste management helps reduce landfill waste, lower pollution, and support a healthier environment.
