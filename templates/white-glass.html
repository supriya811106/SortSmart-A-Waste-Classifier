Your Waste Material is White glass.

White glass waste, also known as clear glass, is commonly found in bottles, jars, and windows. It does not decompose naturally and can remain in the environment for thousands of years if not disposed of properly. Broken glass can cause injuries, and excessive waste in landfills takes up valuable space, contributing to environmental pollution.

Recycling white glass is the best way to manage waste and conserve resources. It can be melted and reused indefinitely without losing quality. Proper disposal through recycling programs helps reduce landfill waste, save energy, and protect natural resources, making it an eco-friendly choice.
